Definition

Artificial body parts are artificial devices that are used to replace or support a person's missing natural body part due to disease, injury or genetic defects. Bionic people are people who have been fitted with artificial body parts. The type of prosthesis a person can use is determined by factors such as the cause of amputation or limb loss, as well as the location of the missing limb.

In our context, Artificial body parts and bionic people are the use of electronics or prosthetics to aid humans in completing day-to-day living that they might not be able to do without.
